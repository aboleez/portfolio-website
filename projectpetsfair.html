PetsFair

My Roles: User Research & UI Designing

About the Project

Adopting a pet is preferable than purchasing one in today's world. However, owning a pet has a lot of responsibilities that must be met, such as finding a dependable pet sitter when needed and caring for your pet's diet, hygiene, and health.This app will help by providing all of the services needed to make pet parenting easy.


Problem

Due to several long-standing misunderstandings regarding pet adoption, people are still more likely to buy a pet than to adopt one. And think, adoption as a challenging task.Absence of understanding about basic animal care, which is just as vital for human.Not being able to locate a veterinary doctor who fulfills the required medical criteria.Finding a local pet store and pet sitters can be tough.


Solution

Offering extensive information, including health information and personality attributes, to assist people in finding their furry friends. Online adopters' document verification helps to ensure the legitimacy and expediency of the adoption procedure. Basic at-home health examination guidelines for a range of problems that would be helpful for all pet owners.Providing personalized healthcare services based on health problems only with very few easy steps and online appointment booking. Recommendations for pet sitters and pet stores depending on location..


Design Process


Competitive Analysis

I studied Pet Bubs, Pet Match, Monkoodog and many similar apps . This helped me to not only understand the competitors but also get inspired and observe the issues in these apps, which I could overcome.

  • Pet Bubs provided facility to connect various other fellow users or the pet service providers; providing emergency numbers, reporting of animal abuse, legal information & forms, police procedures, etc. were the unique features.
  • Monkoodog also provides users with dog training online courses. This feature can be really handy for people who would want to home school their pet.

User Flows

Based on the competitive analysis I have created three user flows. Key features like, Pet Adoption, Pet Health Care and Pet Shops & Sitters

Usability Testing

I have used low-fidelity prototypes to conduct usability test with three users. There were few negative and positive feedbacks. But all in all they were happy with the app flow and could easily go through the app. This helped me improvising on my user experience of the app.


Negative feedback

  • There is no way I could see the pet’s health details, which I would want to know thoroughly and in detail when I am looking at the pet profile
  • I would want to know what document would work for verification in adoption form screen.
  • Reading of terms and conditions is mandatory for me before I proceed to filling the adoption form.
  • Confirmation of appointment booking is not clear.

Positive feedback

  • I just loved the playful and fresh feel of the app.
  • I really liked the feature of primary care and considering my own experience as a pet parent I am sure this would be the most used feature of the app.
  • This app will surely promote pet adoption since it will be really a convenient process henceforth.
